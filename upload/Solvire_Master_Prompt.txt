• No cramped text blocks
• No excessive bolding outside key moments

Ensure logo visibility on both dark and light backgrounds.

ILLUSTRATION STYLE:

SECTION 5 — WEBSITE ARCHITECTURE & NARRATIVE FLOW
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Design the experience as one seamless scrolling journey.
Do not label it like a presentation deck.
Do not make sections feel like hard boxes.
The end of one section should naturally flow into the next.

The visitor should feel like they are DISCOVERING Solvire —
not reading a marketing page.

Narrative arc: curiosity → recognition → understanding →
agreement → trust → action

STORYTELLING SEQUENCE (in order):

01. HERO
Dark Navy background. Oversized headline. One-line sub-copy.
The visitor immediately knows what Solvire is and why it matters.
Primary CTA: Explore Solvire | Secondary CTA: Connect with Us

02. THE PROBLEM (emotional reveal)
Do NOT immediately list services.
Make the visitor feel the problem first.
Story: Solar is installed with excitement. Months pass. Dust
accumulates. Performance drops. People don't know who to call,
when to service, or how to protect their system.
Copy direction: "Solar ownership doesn't end at installation.
The real journey begins afterwards."
Let the visitor think: "I never thought about this."

03. WHY SOLVIRE EXISTS
Only introduce Solvire after the problem lands.
Present Solvire as a calm, thoughtful solution — not another company.
"Solvire was created to simplify everything that happens after
installation."

04. SOLVIRE SERVICES
Present all offerings under the Solvire brand:
• Solvire Cleaning & Maintenance
• Solvire Inspection & Diagnostics
• Solvire AMC (Annual Maintenance Contract)
• Solvire Fault Diagnosis & Repair
• Solvire Protection & Insurance
• Solvire Care Records & History
One strong idea per service card. No clutter.

05. HOW SOLVIRE WORKS
Simple 3–4 step flow:
Search → Compare → Book → Track → Review
Clean process visualization. Trust-first design.

06. TRUST & VERIFICATION LAYER
Show trust early — never bury it in the footer.
Trust signals: Verified badges / Service history / Transparent
process cues / Clear support access / Before/after evidence /
Quality checks / Strong visual hierarchy around credibility

07. PROTECTION & INSURANCE ECOSYSTEM
Premium trust layer. Solar Protection Score concept.
Coverage categories: panels, inverter, battery, wiring,
theft, storm, fire, flood.
Policy vault: one place for documents, records, claims.
Keep it simple and integrated — not a separate insurance portal.

08. TECHNOLOGY & INTELLIGENCE
Show Solvire as a forward-thinking platform.
Smart matching, maintenance reminders, performance tracking,
service history visibility. Calm and technical — not gimmicky.

09. ROADMAP
Show Solvire's expansion journey:
Maintenance → Inspection → AMC → Diagnostics → Protection →
Monitoring → Performance Tracking → Solar Ownership OS
Present as a confident vision. Not a promise list.

10. FOUNDER STORY
Personal, credible, visionary.
Do NOT call it "About Us."
Label it: "Why Solvire Exists" or "Built for Long-Term Solar Care"
Include: founder portrait area, 2–3 line philosophy,
why Solvire was started, what problem the founder noticed,
one powerful belief statement or quote.
Keep it human, editorial, and iconic.

11. FINAL CALL TO ACTION
Strong. Simple. Inviting. Not salesy.
"Explore Solvire" / "Start with Solvire" / "Connect with Solvire"
The ending should feel like an invitation to join a serious movement.

SECTION 7 — MOTION DESIGN SYSTEM
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Motion Philosophy:
Every movement must communicate meaning.
Motion should connect information — not decorate it.
The visitor should NEVER consciously notice the animations.
They should simply feel that everything is incredibly polished.

Motion must feel: Elegant / Calm / Premium / Natural /
Responsive / Confident / Minimal

Motion must NEVER feel: Flashy / Gimmicky / Distracting /
Excessive / Decorative

Preferred Libraries:
• GSAP + ScrollTrigger for scroll-driven animation
• Framer Motion for component-level interactions
• Lenis for smooth, weighted scrolling
• Three.js ONLY if it adds genuine meaningful value

Motion Timing System:
• Hover interactions:   150–250ms
• Button press:         100–150ms
• Card hover:           200–300ms
• Content reveal:       500–700ms
• Section transition:   700–1000ms
• Hero reveal:          1200–1800ms
• Page transition:      600–900ms

Use smooth easing curves. Motion must feel physically believable.

Scroll Choreography:
• Scrolling should feel like exploring a premium product
• Sections should NEVER feel like hard boxes
• Alternate rhythm: Large Statement → Visual → Quiet Space →
  Supporting Copy → Interactive Element → Visual Reveal → Next Experience
• Breathing space is part of the design

Typography Animation:
• Large headings: fade → rise → sharpen → reveal
• Never bounce, overshoot, or rotate dramatically
• Paragraphs appear after headings — maintain reading rhythm

Image Reveals:
• Mask reveals / Soft fade / Blur to focus / Directional light reveal
• Large imagery should feel cinematic

Background Motion:
• Very subtle — light movement, solar rays, soft gradient shift
• Never distracting particle explosions

Button Interactions:
• Hover: small lift + soft glow + gentle scale
• Press: fast, subtle tactile response

Cursor (Desktop only):
• Subtle light glow + magnetic attraction + context awareness
• Keep it understated — never gimmicky

Energy Visual Language:
Use one flowing energy line metaphor throughout the experience.
The same energy line may: reveal content / connect sections /
guide attention / become diagrams / highlight trust / reveal roadmap /
become CTA animation

Loading Screen:
Create a branded intro — not a generic spinner.
Concept: Solar light slowly appears → Energy line grows →
Logo forms from light → Soft grid fades in

Page Entry:
Dark background → Light appears → Brand reveals → Hero loads
Never display everything immediately.
